# Kapture Logistics — Inevitable GTM Strategy

> One sentence: Kapture becomes the default place a UK logistics operator goes when they realise their website is costing them business — by being everywhere they look, with proof they can verify in 24 hours.

## 0. Executive Summary

**The product:** A fully built, deployable Next.js logistics website. Branded for the buyer, live in 24 hours, hosted on Vercel, lead capture wired to Supabase, calendar booking tied to their Google Calendar. Sold by Kapture Studio.

**The wedge:** Logistics SMEs are bleeding leads to better-looking competitors but can't justify £25K-£60K for a custom build. Our offer collapses that decision into a £2,500 — £8,500 deployment that goes live by tomorrow.

**The moat:** A free brand-audit funnel (Kurongeka) that diagnoses the prospect's existing site, hands them a public score and shame list, and offers Kapture Logistics as the prescribed fix. We're the doctor that wrote the diagnosis.

**The economics (target year 1):**
- 10 paid deployments per month × £5,000 average = £600K ARR
- Conversion target: 4% of audited leads → buyers
- Audit funnel target: 250 audits/month → 10 buyers
- Payback period: ≤ 2 weeks (cash-positive on day 1, no advance product cost)

**The 12-month North Star:** £1M ARR, 80% gross margin, 200 logistics websites shipped, ranked #1 on Google UK for *"logistics website template"* and three sister terms.

## 6. The Acquisition Engine — five compounding channels

The strategy is not one channel done well. It's five free channels firing simultaneously, each cross-pollinating the others.

### Channel 1 — Kurongeka audit funnel (the wedge)
**Why first:** Free entry → diagnostic → paid prescription. Every audit produces a brand health score, a public dashboard, and a clear call to action. The dashboard *itself* is a marketing asset — prospects share it, it gets indexed, peers see the format.

**Mechanic:**
1. We audit 50 UK logistics SMEs proactively (no signup required) and publish their scores
2. We email each one their dashboard with subject line *"We audited [Brand]'s digital presence. Here's what we found."*
3. Each dashboard ends with a tile: *"Score below 70? Kapture Logistics ships you a new site in 24 hours →"*
4. Conversion target: 5-10% of audited brands take the discovery call
5. Once we hit 50 published audits, the audit page itself becomes a search asset (prospects search competitor names, find our audit, click through)

**Cost: zero. Just our time.**

### Channel 2 — LinkedIn (organic + outbound)
**Why:** Every Trevor lives on LinkedIn. His CFO does too. So do their competitors.

**Organic plan:**
- Acie posts 4×/week from the Kapture Logistics angle (case study, audit findings, before/after, behind-the-scenes)
- Sarah Imani (or Henrik) posts 2×/week from the operator-marketer angle
- Every post has a clear CTA: *"DM me 'AUDIT' and we'll grade your site free this week."*

**Outbound plan:**
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator free trial → list-build 2,000 UK logistics decision-makers
- Connect → 3-touch sequence (no pitch in the first message)
- Move qualified DMs to email or Calendly

### Channel 3 — SEO + Programmatic content
**Why:** Trevor Googles "freight forwarder website design uk" at 11pm. We need to be the first three results.

**Phase 1 (month 1-3):** Long-tail commercial keywords
- *"logistics website template"*
- *"freight forwarder website design"*
- *"haulage website builder uk"*
- *"3pl website template"*
- *"customs broker website uk"*

**Phase 2 (month 3-6):** Programmatic SEO
- 50 city-specific landing pages: */websites-for-freight-forwarders-felixstowe*, */haulage-websites-manchester*, etc. UK has ~50 logistics hubs. Each page reuses the demo with city-localised copy.

**Phase 3 (month 6+):** Authority content
- *"State of UK Logistics Websites"* — annual report, audited 200 sites, scored, named names. This becomes the citation everybody links to. Massive AEO play.

### Channel 4 — Trade body partnerships
**Why:** Logistics UK has 18,000 members. BIFA has 1,500. RHA has 8,000. Their newsletters are read by Trevor every Tuesday morning.

**Approach:**
- Offer free brand-audit days for each association — we audit 20 random members per association, publish a "Logistics UK Member Digital Health Report"
- Trade for a feature in the member newsletter
- Propose a free webinar: *"Why your website is losing you tenders — and what to do about it"*
- Outcome: trusted-source backlinks + warm referrals

### Channel 5 — Referral + case study flywheel
**Why:** Logistics is a tight community. Trevor talks to 6 other Trevors per week.

**Mechanic:**
- Every shipped client signs a 1-page case study (we write it, they approve)
- Case studies live at `/work/[client]` and link from their site footer "Built by Kapture"
- 15% referral fee paid in cash to anyone who introduces a paid client
- Quarterly Kapture Logistics Slack with all clients — peer-to-peer signal, retention compounds
